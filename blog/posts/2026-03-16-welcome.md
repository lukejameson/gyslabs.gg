# Why I Built GSY Labs

Two years ago I built seas.gg because I wanted to know the sea temperature, check the weather, and figure out when the bathing pools would be underwater due to the tide. The data existed but it was scattered. So I built one place that showed all of it. It sat quietly for a while, but it proved something: Guernsey had good data that just needed a better interface.

Then 3 months ago i built roads.gg. The States had the data and people needed it, but the site was painful on mobile and built on older tech. A better interface made it actually useful. Then a few weeks ago launched airways.gg because flight data deserves context — patterns, weather, performance trends, not just a departure board.

So now we have a few tools.

GSY Labs exists to solve a simple problem: Guernsey has good data. The States publish it, the airport publishes it, we have APIs. But the interfaces are often outdated or designed for a different era. I build tools that present this information the way people actually want to use it — fast, on mobile, without friction.

## What's here

**roads.gg** — Road closures for people who actually need to get places. Search by postcode, road name, filter what matters to you.

**airways.gg** — Flight data with context. Not just departures/arrivals, but delay patterns, weather conditions, historical performance. Built because someone should be able to answer "is Sunday really that bad to fly?" with data.

**seas.gg** — Water temperature, tides, weather, pool status. Started this whole thing.

**routes.gg** — Currently building. Bus timetables that don't feel like they're from 2004. Natural language search, journey planning that works, real-time tracking.

## What I'll write about here

- How I built these things and what worked (and what didn't)
- Data patterns I've found — like why Sunday is the worst day to flight
- The weird technical challenges of building on a small island
- Why this matters beyond just "oh that's neat"

Guernsey is small enough that infrastructure data can actually move the needle. A better way to find your bus route means fewer cars. Knowing road closures means less time wasted. Flight delay patterns help people plan actual trips.
